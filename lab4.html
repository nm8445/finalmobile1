Welcome to Lab 4

In my fourth lab, I went over the concept and usage of objects in programming, focusing on Dart and C#. Objects are instances of classes that bundle variables and methods into a single unit, making them essential for encapsulation, modularity, reusability, inheritance, and real-world modelling. I compared the syntax and features of Dart and C#, noting key differences: Dart supports named constructors and null safety, while C# uses property getters/setters, constructor overloading, and enforces stricter class-level privacy. However, Dart's limitations include the lack of multiple inheritance and library-level privacy enforcement.

To demonstrate objects in practice, I modelled a shopping cart system with two classes: Product and ShoppingCart. The Product class defined attributes like name, price, and quantity, alongside a method to calculate the total price. The ShoppingCart class managed a list of products, allowing additions and displaying the cart's total cost. Through methods like addProduct, getTotalCost, and displayCart, I integrated these classes into a functional system.

Finally, I discussed real-world applications, such as an e-commerce app. In this scenario, objects like Product, User, and Order represent entities with attributes and methods for managing data and interactions. These objects form the backend logic, while the UI, developed in Flutter, interacts with them to display information and handle actions like adding products to the cart and processing orders.
